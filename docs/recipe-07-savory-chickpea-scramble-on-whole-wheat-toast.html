Savory Chickpea Scramble on Whole Wheat Toast

A quick toast-based breakfast with guidance on ripeness, spreading, and timing eggs so everything is ready together.

Serves 1 Method: toast Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. If you are using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them. If you cooked them from dry, make sure they are fully tender before starting the scramble.
  2. Warm a skillet over medium heat with a little oil, then add the chickpeas and any vegetables or seasonings. Mash some of the chickpeas lightly with a fork or spatula so the mixture feels more like scrambled filling than loose beans.
  3. Cook until the chickpeas are hot and the vegetables soften. Add a spoonful of water if the pan looks dry.
  4. Toast the bread while the scramble cooks so both parts finish at about the same time.
  5. Pile the chickpea mixture onto the toast and serve right away.

Ingredients

  • 0.75 cup canned chickpeas, drained
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 0.5 tsp turmeric
  • 0.5 tsp cumin
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 slice whole-wheat toast

Equipment

  • toaster or skillet
  • small bowl
  • fork
  • knife

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: toaster or skillet, small bowl, fork, knife.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.
  • If your recipe uses cooked beans, chickpeas, or lentils, decide whether you are using canned legumes or cooking a batch from dry pantry staples first.

Ingredient notes

  • This is a forgiving recipe. Prepare the ingredients before heating anything, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Pulse chickpeas to a coarse crumb.
  2. Saute with oil, turmeric, cumin, and garlic.
  3. Serve on toast.

How to prepare chickpeas from dry pantry stock

  1. Pick through the dry chickpeas and rinse them well.
  2. Soak them overnight in plenty of water. Chickpeas expand a lot, so use a large bowl.
  3. Drain, transfer to a pot, and cover with fresh water by about 2 inches.
  4. Bring to a boil, lower to a simmer, and cook until very tender. This usually takes about 60 to 90 minutes on the stove after soaking.
  5. Taste one. It should be creamy inside, not chalky or crunchy.
  6. Drain and cool. Freeze extra chickpeas in 1/2-cup bags or containers so future breakfasts are much easier.

If something goes wrong

  • Legumes still tough: they simply need more cooking time. Simmer longer and check every 10 minutes.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
