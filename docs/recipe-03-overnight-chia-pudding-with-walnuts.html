Overnight Chia Pudding with Walnuts

A straightforward breakfast recipe rewritten with extra detail for cooks who want everything explained clearly.

Serves 1 Method: no cook Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Put all measured ingredients into a jar or bowl large enough for stirring without spilling.
  2. Stir slowly, scraping the bottom and sides, until the seeds or oats are evenly coated and no dry pockets remain.
  3. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes, then stir again. This second stir breaks up clumps and helps everything thicken evenly.
  4. Cover and chill if the recipe is meant to be overnight, or let it stand on the counter until thick if it is a quick pudding.
  5. Taste before serving and add a tiny splash of liquid if the mixture became thicker than you like.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 cup unsweetened soy milk
  • 0.25 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp walnuts, chopped

Equipment

  • jar or bowl
  • spoon

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: jar or bowl, spoon.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • This is a forgiving recipe. Prepare the ingredients before heating anything, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Mix chia seeds, soy milk, and vanilla; refrigerate overnight.
  2. Top with chopped walnuts in the morning.

If something goes wrong

  • Too thick: add a small splash of milk or water and stir or blend again.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
