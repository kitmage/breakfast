Chickpea and Spinach Breakfast Burrito

A portable breakfast with simple rolling instructions and help for warming fillings so the tortilla does not tear.

Serves 1 Method: burrito Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Warm the filling first. Cold beans or vegetables make the burrito feel heavy and can tear the tortilla.
  2. Heat the tortilla for 10 to 20 seconds in the microwave or a dry skillet so it becomes flexible.
  3. Place the tortilla on a plate and arrange the filling in a short line slightly below the center. Do not overfill it.
  4. Fold the left and right sides inward, then roll from the bottom upward while tucking the filling in tightly.
  5. If you want a firmer burrito, place it seam-side down in a dry skillet for 1 to 2 minutes so it lightly toasts and holds together.

Ingredients

  • 1 large whole-wheat wrap
  • 0.75 cup chickpea scramble
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 0.25 medium avocado, sliced

Equipment

  • skillet or microwave
  • spoon
  • plate

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: skillet or microwave, spoon, plate.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • A ripe avocado should give slightly when you press the skin. If it feels rock hard, leave it on the counter for another day or two.
  • Cut vegetables into small, similar pieces so they soften at the same speed and are easier to chew in a breakfast dish.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Fill wrap with warm chickpea scramble, spinach, and avocado.
  2. Wrap and serve.

How to prepare chickpeas from dry pantry stock

  1. Pick through the dry chickpeas and rinse them well.
  2. Soak them overnight in plenty of water. Chickpeas expand a lot, so use a large bowl.
  3. Drain, transfer to a pot, and cover with fresh water by about 2 inches.
  4. Bring to a boil, lower to a simmer, and cook until very tender. This usually takes about 60 to 90 minutes on the stove after soaking.
  5. Taste one. It should be creamy inside, not chalky or crunchy.
  6. Drain and cool. Freeze extra chickpeas in 1/2-cup bags or containers so future breakfasts are much easier.

If something goes wrong

  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
