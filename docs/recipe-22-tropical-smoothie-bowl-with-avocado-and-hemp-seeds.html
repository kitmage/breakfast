Tropical Smoothie Bowl with Avocado and Hemp Seeds

A fast blender breakfast with clear tips on texture, layering ingredients, and making frozen fruit work for you.

Serves 1 Method: blender Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Add liquid to the blender first. This helps the blades move more easily once the frozen ingredients go in.
  2. Add soft ingredients next, then frozen fruit or ice last.
  3. Blend on low for a few seconds, then increase to high until smooth. Stop and scrape down the sides if pieces get stuck.
  4. If the smoothie is too thick to blend, add 1 to 2 tablespoons of liquid at a time instead of pouring in a lot at once.
  5. Taste before serving and adjust sweetness or thickness as needed.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup frozen mango
  • 0.5 cup frozen pineapple
  • 0.25 medium avocado
  • 0.5 cup coconut water
  • 1 tbsp hemp seeds

Equipment

  • blender
  • measuring cup
  • spoon

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: blender, measuring cup, spoon.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • A ripe avocado should give slightly when you press the skin. If it feels rock hard, leave it on the counter for another day or two.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Blend fruit, avocado, and coconut water.
  2. Serve in a bowl and top with hemp seeds.

If something goes wrong

  • Too thick: add a small splash of milk or water and stir or blend again.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
