Veggie Omelet with Sterol-Fortified Orange Juice

A cooked breakfast with step-by-step heat control and sequencing notes so each ingredient finishes at the right time.

Serves 1 Method: omelet Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Beat the eggs in a bowl with a fork until the yolks and whites look fully combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium-low heat and add a little oil or butter. The pan should feel hot but not smoke.
  3. Cook any vegetables first until they soften. Wet vegetables can tear an omelet if they are added raw.
  4. Pour in the eggs and let them sit briefly, then use a spatula to gently pull cooked egg from the edges toward the center.
  5. When the top still looks a little moist but not raw, add fillings to one half and fold the omelet over. Rest for 30 to 60 seconds before sliding onto a plate.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 0.5 cup mixed vegetables, diced
  • 1 serving olive oil spray
  • 4 oz sterol-fortified orange juice

Equipment

  • nonstick skillet
  • spatula
  • bowl
  • fork

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: nonstick skillet, spatula, bowl, fork.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • Crack eggs on a flat counter, not on the bowl edge. This reduces shell pieces and keeps the yolk more intact.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Cook omelet with egg, egg whites, and vegetables using olive oil spray.
  2. Serve with sterol-fortified orange juice.

If something goes wrong

  • Eggs browning too fast: lower the heat. Eggs cook better gently than quickly.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
