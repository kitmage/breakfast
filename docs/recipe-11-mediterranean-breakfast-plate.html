Mediterranean Breakfast Plate

A straightforward breakfast recipe rewritten with extra detail for cooks who want everything explained clearly.

Serves 1 Method: assemble Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Wash and slice any fresh vegetables or fruit first so the plate is easy to assemble once you start.
  2. Toast bread or warm any grain component if you want part of the plate served hot.
  3. Portion protein items such as yogurt, eggs, beans, or cheese into small piles or bowls instead of mixing everything together.
  4. Arrange the plate with larger items first, then tuck smaller items into open spaces. This keeps the plate neat and easy to eat.
  5. Add a final drizzle of olive oil, lemon, herbs, or seasoning and serve.

Ingredients

  • 0.25 cup hummus
  • 6-8 pieces olives
  • 0.5 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 0.5 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 0.25 pita whole-wheat pita

Equipment

  • knife
  • plate
  • small bowl

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: knife, plate, small bowl.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • This is a forgiving recipe. Prepare the ingredients before heating anything, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Arrange all ingredients on a plate and serve.

If something goes wrong

  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
