Whole Wheat Blender Waffles with Raspberry Compote

A cooked breakfast with step-by-step heat control and sequencing notes so each ingredient finishes at the right time.

Serves 1 Method: waffles Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Preheat the waffle iron fully. Most waffle irons cook better when they are completely hot before batter goes in.
  2. Blend or whisk the batter until smooth, then let it sit for 2 to 3 minutes so bubbles settle and dry ingredients hydrate.
  3. Grease the waffle iron lightly if your model tends to stick.
  4. Pour in enough batter to cover most of the grid without overflowing, then close the lid and let the waffle cook until the iron signals or steam decreases a lot.
  5. Lift the waffle out gently with a fork or silicone tool and let it rest for 1 minute before topping so it stays crisp.

Ingredients

  • 0.75 cup whole-wheat flour
  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 0.75 cup milk of choice
  • 1 cup frozen raspberries
  • 1 tsp honey

Equipment

  • blender
  • waffle iron
  • measuring cups

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: blender, waffle iron, measuring cups.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • If you are using frozen fruit, you do not need to thaw it for hot oatmeal or smoothies. For toast or bowls, thaw just enough so it is easy to eat.
  • Crack eggs on a flat counter, not on the bowl edge. This reduces shell pieces and keeps the yolk more intact.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Blend waffle ingredients and cook in waffle iron.
  2. Simmer raspberries with honey to make compote.
  3. Top waffles with compote.

If something goes wrong

  • Eggs browning too fast: lower the heat. Eggs cook better gently than quickly.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
