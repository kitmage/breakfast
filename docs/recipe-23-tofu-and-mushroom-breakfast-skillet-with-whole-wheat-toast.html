Day 23

Tofu and Mushroom Breakfast Skillet with Whole Wheat Toast

A quick toast-based breakfast with guidance on ripeness, spreading, and timing eggs so everything is ready together.

Serves 1 Method: toast Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Toast the bread until the surface is crisp and lightly browned. If you do not own a toaster, warm the bread in a dry skillet over medium heat and flip once.
  2. While the bread toasts, prepare the topping in a small bowl. Mash avocado, beans, or other spreadable ingredients with a fork until they are easy to spread.
  3. If the recipe includes an egg, cook it while the bread finishes. Use medium heat so the egg cooks gently instead of burning.
  4. Spread the topping over the hot toast all the way to the edges so each bite has flavor.
  5. Finish with seasonings, fruit, or crunchy toppings and serve right away so the toast stays crisp.

Ingredients

  • 0.5 block firm tofu
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 0.5 cup onion, sliced
  • 0.25 tsp black salt (kala namak)
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 slice whole-wheat toast

Equipment

  • toaster or skillet
  • small bowl
  • fork
  • knife

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: toaster or skillet, small bowl, fork, knife.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • If the tofu looks very wet, wrap it in paper towels and gently press for 5 to 10 minutes so the final dish tastes less watery.
  • Cut vegetables into small, similar pieces so they soften at the same speed and are easier to chew in a breakfast dish.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Saute mushrooms and onion in oil.
  2. Add crumbled tofu and black salt; cook through.
  3. Serve with toast.

If something goes wrong

  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
