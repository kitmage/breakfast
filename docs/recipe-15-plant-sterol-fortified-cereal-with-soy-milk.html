Day 15

Plant Sterol-Fortified Cereal with Soy Milk

A straightforward breakfast recipe rewritten with extra detail for cooks who want everything explained clearly.

Serves 1 Method: assemble Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Place the cereal in a bowl first so you can judge how much milk the bowl will comfortably hold.
  2. Pour in the soy milk slowly. Add less than you think at first because you can always add more.
  3. Slice banana or add fruit on top rather than under the cereal so it stays visible and is easier to eat.
  4. Stir just once or twice if you want every bite slightly coated, or leave it layered if you prefer crunch.
  5. Eat promptly so the cereal does not become soggy.

Ingredients

  • 1 serving whole-grain sterol-fortified cereal
  • 1 cup unsweetened soy milk
  • 1 medium banana, sliced

Equipment

  • bowl
  • spoon

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: bowl, spoon.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • If you are using frozen fruit, you do not need to thaw it for hot oatmeal or smoothies. For toast or bowls, thaw just enough so it is easy to eat.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Pour cereal into bowl, add soy milk, and top with banana slices.

If something goes wrong

  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
