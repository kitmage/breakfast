Day 27

Savory Oat and Lentil Porridge with Nutritional Yeast

A warm bowl-style breakfast with beginner-friendly stovetop instructions and texture cues to tell when grains are done.

Serves 1 Method: simmer Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Bring the water or milk to a gentle boil, then lower the heat so the pot simmers instead of violently bubbling.
  2. Stir in the grains slowly while stirring. This reduces clumping and helps the texture stay even.
  3. Cook on low to medium-low heat, stirring every few minutes and scraping the bottom of the pot so nothing sticks.
  4. If the mixture becomes thicker than expected before the grains soften, add a splash of hot water and keep cooking.
  5. When the grains are tender, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let it sit for 2 to 5 minutes. This short rest improves texture.

Ingredients

  • 0.5 cup red lentils
  • 0.5 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 2 tbsp green onions, sliced
  • 1 tsp hot sauce

Equipment

  • small pot
  • spoon
  • measuring cups

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: small pot, spoon, measuring cups.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.
  • If your recipe uses cooked beans, chickpeas, or lentils, decide whether you are using canned legumes or cooking a batch from dry pantry staples first.

Ingredient notes

  • Grains are done when they taste tender, not crunchy. If the pan looks dry before the grains soften, add a splash more water.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Simmer lentils and oats in broth until creamy.
  2. Stir in nutritional yeast; top with green onions and hot sauce.

How to prepare lentils from dry pantry stock

  1. Lentils are easier than most dried beans because they usually do not need soaking.
  2. Spread the lentils on a plate and look for small stones or wrinkled lentils, then rinse well.
  3. Put 1 cup lentils and 3 cups water in a pot. Bring to a boil, then lower to a gentle simmer.
  4. Cook until tender. Brown or green lentils usually take 20 to 35 minutes; red lentils cook faster and become soft and creamy.
  5. Drain any extra liquid if needed. Lentils should be soft enough to bite easily without crunching.

If something goes wrong

  • Too thick: stir in a spoonful of hot water at a time until the texture loosens.
  • Legumes still tough: they simply need more cooking time. Simmer longer and check every 10 minutes.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
