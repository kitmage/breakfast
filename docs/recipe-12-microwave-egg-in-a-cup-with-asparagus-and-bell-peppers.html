Day 12

Microwave Egg in a Cup with Asparagus and Bell Peppers

A cooked breakfast with step-by-step heat control and sequencing notes so each ingredient finishes at the right time.

Serves 1 Method: assemble Tags: whole-grains, produce, protein

Beginner-friendly instructions

  1. Grease a microwave-safe mug or bowl lightly so the egg releases more easily after cooking.
  2. Use a microwave-safe mug or bowl that is larger than the ingredients. Eggs puff up as they cook, and extra space prevents overflow.
  3. Cut the vegetables into very small pieces so they soften during the short microwave time.
  4. Beat the egg in the mug with a fork until the color looks even, then stir in the vegetables and seasonings.
  5. Microwave in short bursts, stirring between bursts, until the egg is set but still moist. Short bursts prevent rubbery eggs.
  6. Let the mug stand for 1 minute before eating because microwaved eggs stay very hot after the microwave stops.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 0.25 cup asparagus, chopped
  • 0.25 cup bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 slice whole-wheat toast
  • 1 tsp plant-sterol margarine

Equipment

  • bowl
  • spoon
  • plate

Before you start

  • Set out your equipment first: bowl, spoon, plate.
  • Read every step one time before you turn on the heat so nothing feels rushed.
  • Wash produce, dry it if needed, and measure ingredients into small bowls or cups before cooking.

Ingredient notes

  • Crack eggs on a flat counter, not on the bowl edge. This reduces shell pieces and keeps the yolk more intact.
  • Cut vegetables into small, similar pieces so they soften at the same speed and are easier to chew in a breakfast dish.

Original recipe card summary

  1. Whisk egg, egg whites, and vegetables in a mug.
  2. Microwave about 90 seconds until set.
  3. Serve with toast and sterol spread.

If something goes wrong

  • Eggs browning too fast: lower the heat. Eggs cook better gently than quickly.
  • Not flavorful enough: add a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, or a dusting of your favorite spice blend.

Make-ahead and leftovers

Most cooked grains, beans, and chopped fruit can be prepared ahead. Store leftovers in covered containers. If texture changes after chilling, add a splash of water or milk and warm or stir again before eating.
